Robert Gagnon Obituary

It is with deep sadness that we announce the passing of Robert John Gagnon, a beloved resident of Eau Claire. On Monday, November 13, Robert’s life was tragically cut short at the age of 65. His sudden departure has left a void in the hearts of all who knew him.

Death of Robert Gagnon

The loss of Robert Gagnon has left a profound impact on the Eau Claire community. His vibrant spirit and warm presence will be dearly missed by his family, friends, and all those whose lives he touched. As we mourn his passing, let us remember the joy and happiness he brought to the world.

Early Life and Education

Robert John Gagnon was born on June 23, 1958, in Eau Claire to James and Monica Gagnon. He grew up in a loving family and received his education at Regis High School, graduating in 1977. Continuing his academic journey, Robert attended the prestigious University of Notre Dame from 1977 to 1982, where he pursued his passion for learning.

Achievements in Football

Robert’s athletic prowess was evident from a young age. He excelled in football and was recognized as the 1976 Wisconsin Football Player of the Year. His exceptional skills led him to become an All-American player, representing his school and community with pride. Notably, Robert played as an offensive guard for the Fighting Irish, contributing to their National Championship victory in his rookie year.

Professional Life and Entrepreneurship

After his successful football career, Robert ventured into the world of business. He worked at LA Revenue until 2014 before embarking on a new path as a wellness entrepreneur. With a passion for promoting health and well-being, Robert dedicated himself to helping others lead fulfilling lives. His entrepreneurial spirit and dedication to his craft were truly inspiring.

Marriage and Personal Interests

In 2016, Robert found love and companionship when he met Jill Thornton. Their bond grew stronger over time, leading them to exchange vows on May 13 at Chetek’s Back of the Moon Campground, a place they cherished for its natural beauty. Robert had a zest for life and enjoyed various activities such as fishing, boating, hunting, and attending concerts. These hobbies brought him immense joy and allowed him to create lasting memories with his loved ones.

Family and Survivors

Robert Gagnon leaves behind a loving family who will forever cherish his memory. He is survived by his wife, Jill, his daughter McKenzie (Trey) Boettcher, and his grandchild Shaye Boettcher. He is also survived by his mother, Monica, and his siblings James, Mary Ellen, Paul, Peter, Michael, and Patrick. Robert’s father, James, his brother David, niece Traci, and nephew Jesse preceded him in death. He will be deeply missed by his extended family, including his father- and mother-in-law, Bryce and Jeanine Cheatham Jr., and his brother-in-law Jeff Cheatham and his wife Mylinda.

A Christian burial will be held on November 28 at Immaculate Conception Parish in Eau Claire, officiated by Fr. Francis Thadathil. Prior to the burial, visitation will take place at Stokes, Prock & Mundt Funeral Chapel in Altoona on November 27. Robert will be laid to rest at Forest Hill Cemetery in Eau Claire. In lieu of flowers, the family kindly requests donations to be made to the Eau Claire County Humane Association, a cause close to Robert’s heart.

Funeral Arrangements

The funeral arrangements for Robert Gagnon have been carefully organized to honor his life and provide an opportunity for loved ones to come together and pay their respects. It is a time to reflect on the memories shared and celebrate the impact Robert had on the lives of those around him.

Christian Burial

On November 28, a Christian burial will take place at Immaculate Conception Parish, located at 1712 Highland Ave. in Eau Claire. This solemn ceremony will be officiated by Fr. Francis Thadathil, who will guide the congregation in honoring Robert’s life and offering prayers for his eternal rest.

Visitation and Mass

Prior to the burial, there will be a visitation held at Stokes, Prock & Mundt Funeral Chapel, located at 535 S. Hillcrest Parkway in Altoona, on November 27. This will provide an opportunity for family, friends, and members of the community to come together and offer their condolences to Robert’s loved ones. The visitation will be followed by a mass at the church on the same day, starting at 10 a.m.

Burial

Following the Christian burial service, Robert Gagnon will be laid to rest at Forest Hill Cemetery in Eau Claire. This serene and peaceful resting place will serve as a final tribute to Robert’s life and provide a space for loved ones to visit and remember him in the years to come.

Donations

In honor of Robert Gagnon’s memory and his passion for making a difference, the family kindly requests that instead of flowers, donations be made to the Eau Claire County Humane Association. This organization holds a special place in Robert’s heart, as he believed in the importance of caring for and protecting animals in need.

By making a donation to the Eau Claire County Humane Association, you will be contributing to their mission of providing shelter, care, and support to homeless and neglected animals. Your generosity will help ensure that these animals receive the love, attention, and medical care they deserve while they wait to find their forever homes.

Donating in Robert’s name is a meaningful way to honor his memory and continue his legacy of compassion and kindness. Your contribution will make a lasting impact on the lives of animals in need, bringing comfort and hope to those who have been abandoned or mistreated.
