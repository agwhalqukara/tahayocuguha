Anne Trujillo Biography

Anne Trujillo is an American journalist working as a broadcast news anchor at Denver 7. She began by working as a general assignment reporter. In 2006, she was named Best Anchor by Westword Magazine. Anne has worked at Denver 7 for at least 30 years. This has made her the longest-tenured TV news anchor in Denver.

Anne was a leadership council member at Escala de Guadalupe and a former board member at Mi Casa Resource Center. Moreover, she was named to the Silver Circle of NATAS National Academy of Television art and science. Currently, she has not shared any information about her retirement.

Moreover, she went to Littleton High School where she later graduated. Later, she joined the University of Colorado at Boulder where she graduated with a bachelor of science degree in broadcast journalism. Additionally, she is the co-president of the Colorado Chapter of the National Association of Hispanic Journalists.

While she was working, she covered the three Bronco Super Bowl appearances, the Columbine and Aurora theater shootings, and the Democratic National Convention in Denver. Sadly, she was diagnosed with a disorder that causes hair loss known as alopecia areata. She has visited various dermatologists and has finally got diagnosed.
