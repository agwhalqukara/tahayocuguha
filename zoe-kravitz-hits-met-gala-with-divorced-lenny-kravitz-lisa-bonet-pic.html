Zoe Kravitz Hits Met Gala With Divorced Lenny Kravitz, Lisa Bonet: Pic

September 2024 · 2 minute read

One swanky family affair! Zoe Kravitz turned heads at the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute Gala on Monday, May 4, and not just for her daring red carpet gown. 

The Divergent actress, 26, was joined on Manhattan’s Upper East Side by her gorgeous parents, Lenny Kravitz and Lisa Bonet. Though they divorced more than 20 years ago, the musician and the actress happily cozied up to one another at the China: Through The Looking Glass-themed gala. 

All three stars were dressed to the nines for the high-fashion affair. Zoe showed some skin in a custom chainmail Alexander Wang gown with Swarovski crystal embellishment and Chopard jewels. She copied her mother’s trademark braids and drew attention to her eyes with dark eyeliner. 

Bonet, 47, and Lenny, 50, coordinated in chic Valentino looks. The Cosby Show alum sported a long-sleeved floral gown from the designer’s Spring 2015 Haute Couture collection, while the Grammy winner donned Valentino Garavani sunglasses and a navy pinstripe Valentino suit. 

The “Fly Away” singer took to Instagram on Monday night to share a sweet photo with his ex-wife and daughter. The three snuggle up for a selfie, standing close and looking gorgeous in their formal wear. 

“#MetBall2015,” Lenny captioned the shot. “Family.”

The famed performer wed Bonet in November 1987. The two eloped in Las Vegas and went on to welcome daughter Zoe in December 1988. They parted ways in 1993 but remained amicable. Bonet went on to marry Game of Thrones star Jason Momoa, and welcomed a son Nakoa-Wolf, now 6, and daughter Lola, now 7, with the actor. Lenny has not been married again, but dated Vanessa Paradis in the 1990s and Nicole Kidman in the mid-2000s, and was engaged for a short time to Adriana Lima in the early 2000s. 

“I think I’m lucky having parents that have been in show business for a while and they don’t care about the shiny stuff so much,” Zoe told Indiewire in December 2012 of her parents. “They raised me in that way — to stay grounded, not to chase the shiny pretty things. I stay in the moment, because when you do that the hype goes away.”
